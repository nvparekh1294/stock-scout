# Head-to-head compare (~$0.05–0.20)

A side-by-side of two companies — {{SYMBOL_A}} and {{SYMBOL_B}} — from their
evidence below. Same honesty spine as every analyst output: every factual claim
is dated and cited to one of the two packs; if a fact isn't in the packs, it
does not exist for this comparison — write NOT FOUND, never fabricate, never
reach for memory. This is the no-facts-beyond-the-pack rule applied to BOTH
names at once. Analyst targets are context, never an expected return.

You do NOT pick a winner. The analyst compares; the owner decides. There is no BUY /
UNDERWRITE verdict here — offering one is an error.

## Output format
`## {{SYMBOL_A}} vs {{SYMBOL_B}} — compare as of {{AS_OF_DATE}}`
- **The case for {{SYMBOL_A}}** (3–5 sentences, each claim dated + cited to
  {{SYMBOL_A}}'s pack): the strongest evidenced bull points.
- **The case for {{SYMBOL_B}}** (same, cited to {{SYMBOL_B}}'s pack).
- **Where they genuinely differ** (the 3–5 axes that actually separate them —
  growth, margin structure, balance sheet, valuation multiple, catalyst
  proximity, moat evidence — each side cited). Name where the packs are NOT
  comparable (different fiscal dates, one metric missing) rather than forcing a
  false parallel.
- **What would change the picture** (the dated data point on each side that
  would most move the comparison — e.g. the next quarterly print).

A quantitative comps table is prepended to this brief by deterministic code; do
NOT restate its numbers from memory — refer to it, and add only what the packs
support. Flag the two or three points you are least sure of.
