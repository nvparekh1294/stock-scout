# Quick take (radar default, ~$0.10–0.30)

A fast, cheap read on one company from the evidence pack below. Same honesty
spine as the full underwrite — every claim dated and cited, never fabricate, and
if there is no provable unpriced edge, say "nothing provable" rather than invent
one. Shorter than a full underwrite: this is triage, not a thesis. Write plain
English for a reader who has never traded: the FIRST time you use any trading,
accounting, or finance term, add a short explanation in parentheses (e.g. "8-K
(a company's official announcement filing)", "forward P/E (share price ÷ next
year's expected earnings per share)") — no unexplained acronyms.

## Output format (keep it tight)
Header: `## {{SYMBOL}} — quick take as of {{AS_OF_DATE}}`
- **Stage & direction:** 0–3 with a re-rating / stable / de-rating marker.
- **Read:** UNDERWRITE-CANDIDATE | WATCH | PASS. The three must mean different
  things: use **WATCH** ONLY if you can name a concrete trigger on the
  `Watching for:` line below (a specific event, number, or date) — no concrete
  trigger and no thesis → **PASS**, not WATCH. **UNDERWRITE-CANDIDATE** means an
  early, evidenced sign it may be mispriced, worth a deeper look (not a confirmed
  underwrite). **PASS** — nothing here suggests the price is wrong; a respectable,
  common finding.
- **Conviction:** `N/5` — 1 = weak evidence, mostly unknowns · 3 = decent
  evidence but no clear edge · 5 = strong, specific, checked evidence.
- **Watching for (required if WATCH):** the one concrete trigger you are waiting
  for — a specific event, number, or date.
- **What might be unpriced (cited) — or "nothing provable":** one or two
  sentences, each fact dated.
- **3 most decision-relevant facts (dated, cited).**
- **One valuation line:** what today's price roughly assumes (multiples from the
  pack; no full scenario tree at this tier).
- **Biggest risk / most likely way this is already priced (one line, cited).**
- **Expression (one line, only if the pack has an options snapshot):** would a
  defined-risk structure (long-dated call / protective put) fit this thesis
  better than stock — binary outcome or Stage 0–1 asymmetry — or not? Defined-
  risk only; never short volatility; analysis, never an order.
- **Worth a full deep-dive? Yes/No — <one plain sentence why>.** Replaces the old
  escalation note: say plainly whether a fuller, more expensive analysis (standard
  dive or full underwrite) is warranted, and why. Do not escalate spend without
  flagging the cost.
- **Bottom line:** the LAST line — 2 to 4 sentences in plain English, no jargon,
  for a smart reader who has never traded: (1) does the evidence lean positive,
  negative, or neutral; (2) the single biggest reason; (3) what would change the
  view.

Hard rule (2026-07-12): never assert a fact about ANY company — subject or peer
— that is not in the pack. A peer you remember but the pack doesn't document
does not exist for this take.
